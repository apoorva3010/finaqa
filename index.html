Unbiased Advice (Individuals)

We value your time. We understand your needs. We are aware you need to select financial products and services from a plethora of options, whether it is insurance or investment products. We do not promote any particular brand or product.

Unbiased Advice (individuals) - Rs. 200/ per question

Answers to your queries about topics/issues that are pain points for you with regard Investments and Insurance. E.g.: -

  • Query: I have been investing Rs. 5000/- per month in XYZ Debt Mutual fund for past 3 years. Accumulated amount is Rs. 2,09,438. I am not sure if it is enough for creating the kind of amount (Rs. 5 lakhs) that I need 2 years down the line. Also the fund was performing earlier but of late it has shown a down turn. Current rate of return is 5%. What should I do?
  • Answer: If you continue with the fund with the SIP amount of Rs. 5000/- you will not be able to achieve the 5 lakhs target 2 years from now. The options you have are:
    • Increase the amount of SIPs to Rs. 10000/- in the XYZ Debt fund for the remaining 2 years. Its non-performance may be temporary and it could pick up eventually. Take indexation benefit for capital gains at the time of withdrawal.
    • Withdraw the amount (take indexation benefit since it is already 3 years) and park it in ABC Arbitrage fund and start SIP in the same for Rs. 10000/- for the remaining 2 years. Gains in a Arbitrage funds will be taxed at 10% for the amount over and above 1 Lakh.

Tax & Estate Advice

Take experts’ guidance on tax planning. Protect your wealth and secure the future of the next generation.

Tax & Estate Advice (individuals) – Rs. 200/- per question

What to expect

  • Answers to your queries about topics/issues that are pain points for you with regard Tax planning or Estate planning. E.g.: -
    • Query: My mother has some Jewellery (family heirlooms), some of which she would like to give me, but my sister in law is adamant that since she is the daughter in law she is the rightful owner for all of the Jewellery. This is creating a lot of friction in the family. Please advise.
    • Answer: Since it your mother’s ‘stridhan’ she has all the right to do with it as she pleases. As per the Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act 2005, a daughter has equal right in her parents’ property. It is recommended that your mother make a Will. The Will can be made on a plain paper but a few things need to be taken in to account:
      • Get the Jewellery valued from a govt. valuer to ascertain its value in Indian Rupees.
      • A medical certificate from a doctor that your mother is of sound mind & health.
      • Your mother should state that the will not made under coercion, threat or undue influence.
      • Attach photographs of the Jewellery to the Will for better understanding and description.
      • Though it is not compulsory it is advised that you register the Will with the registrar’s office.
      • You will need 2 witnesses.
